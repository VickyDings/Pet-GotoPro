Welcome to the bird care guides section of Pet-GoToPro. Where our [bird product reviews](/reviews/birds/) help you choose specific cages, perches, and toys, our guides address the deeper questions of bird husbandry — from species selection all the way through to long-term behavioral enrichment and nutrition.

## Why bird guides matter specifically

Pet birds live remarkably long lives when properly cared for. A well-kept cockatiel can reach twenty years, an African grey can outlive its original owner, and macaws commonly reach fifty years or more. These lifespans mean that bird care decisions compound over decades in ways that dog or cat decisions rarely do. A nutritional mistake that produces mild consequences over a three-year hamster lifespan can produce serious disease over a forty-year cockatoo lifespan. We want our bird guides to reflect that time horizon.

## What we are writing toward

Our priority topics include species-specific care sheets for the most commonly kept companion birds, starting with budgies, cockatiels, conures, and African greys. Each care sheet will cover housing requirements, social needs, dietary recommendations, and the behavioral patterns that signal a well-kept bird versus one whose welfare needs are being missed. Nutrition guides comparing pellet diets, fresh food feeding, and the seeds-only diets still commonly pushed by pet stores come next, because diet is one of the most consequential daily decisions bird parents make. Behavioral enrichment guides round out the initial list, covering foraging opportunities, training basics, and the environmental complexity that keeps captive birds mentally healthy.

## Vet-reviewed content for health topics

Any guide touching on avian health will be reviewed by an avian veterinarian before publication. Avian medicine is a specialized field, and general veterinary knowledge does not always translate well to birds, so we rely on specific expertise rather than generic sources.

## Suggest topics

If you keep birds and have topics you would like us to address, email contact@pet-gotopro.com. Experienced bird keepers often identify gaps in available resources that newcomers cannot yet articulate, and those gap-filling articles are exactly the kind of content we most want to produce.
