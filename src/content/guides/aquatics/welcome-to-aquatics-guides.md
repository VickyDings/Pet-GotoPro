Welcome to the aquatics care guides section of Pet-GoToPro. While our [aquatic product reviews](/reviews/aquatics/) help you equip your tank, our guides address the husbandry knowledge that determines whether that equipment actually produces a thriving aquatic ecosystem.

## Why aquarium keeping needs careful guidance

The difference between a beginner aquarist whose fish die within weeks and one whose tank thrives for years often comes down to a handful of concepts that commercial aquarium retailers fail to communicate clearly. Beginners leave pet stores with a tank, a fish, and a few incorrect assumptions. Those assumptions cause predictable failures — ammonia spikes because the tank was never cycled, stocking choices that create inevitable conflict, disease outbreaks from overcrowded or stressed fish — and many new aquarists conclude that they are bad at fish keeping and quit the hobby rather than recognizing that they were set up to fail.

Our goal with this section is to produce the resources we wish had existed when we started keeping fish, so fewer beginners give up on a hobby that becomes deeply rewarding once the fundamentals are understood.

## What we are writing toward

Our initial priority topics cover the knowledge gaps most responsible for new-aquarist failures. Tank cycling comes first because cycling is the single most important concept in freshwater keeping and the one most commonly misunderstood. We will cover both fish-in and fishless cycling methods with honest attention to the tradeoffs of each. Stocking guides for common tank sizes come next, because appropriate stocking is one of the decisions pet stores routinely get wrong. Disease identification and treatment guides follow, because the ability to recognize ich, velvet, fin rot, and similar problems early dramatically changes outcomes. Planted tank basics round out the initial list, covering lighting, substrate, CO2 supplementation, and plant selection for beginners.

## Saltwater and specialized keeping

Saltwater content is on the roadmap but will build more slowly because the complexity and stakes of reef keeping demand more careful preparation. We will start with marine basics before moving to reef-specific topics like coral selection, flow requirements, and nutrient management.

## Share your tank stories

Experienced aquarists know things about specific fish, plant, and invertebrate species that no generalist source can match. If you keep aquariums and have experiences worth sharing, especially failures and recoveries that taught you something important, email contact@pet-gotopro.com. Real stories from real tanks make for better guides than pure technical exposition.
