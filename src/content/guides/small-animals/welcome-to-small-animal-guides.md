Welcome to the small animals care guides section of Pet-GoToPro. Where our [small animal product reviews](/reviews/small-animals/) help you equip appropriate habitats, our guides tackle the husbandry knowledge that determines whether those habitats actually produce healthy, behaviorally fulfilled pets.

## Why small animal care is often poorly served

Small animals suffer from a particular kind of information gap. They are marketed as "starter pets," which sets up the expectation that their care is simple and that pet store employees can provide adequate guidance. Neither assumption holds up. Guinea pigs require social groups, specific dietary vitamin C supplementation, and enclosure space far exceeding what pet stores typically recommend. Rabbits are complex prey animals whose welfare needs were historically misunderstood even by veterinarians. Hamsters need enclosures many times larger than commonly sold cages, solid-walled wheels of specific diameters, and species-appropriate substrate depths that allow burrowing. The result is a category where the most enthusiastic new keepers often receive the worst advice, because the advice they receive comes from people whose job is to sell products rather than teach care.

We want our small animal guides to reflect current welfare research honestly, even when that means directly contradicting what new keepers have been told by sources they trusted.

## What we are writing toward

Our initial priority topics cover the foundational welfare decisions for each commonly kept species. Guinea pig housing guides come first, because the gap between what guinea pigs actually need and what pet stores commonly sell is particularly large. Rabbit-proofing and free-roaming setup content follows, because modern rabbit welfare guidance supports free-roaming or large pen configurations rather than cages, and most new rabbit keepers do not know this. Syrian hamster housing addresses the 600-square-inch floor space minimum that research has established, along with wheel sizing, substrate depth, and enrichment requirements. Diet guides for each species cover the hay, vegetable, and pellet balance that supports long-term digestive health.

## Honest advice even when uncomfortable

Some of our recommendations in this section will contradict what pet store employees commonly say and what beginner care sheets from commercial sources commonly publish. This is not because we enjoy being contrarian but because the welfare research in this category has advanced meaningfully in recent years while commercial advice has lagged. When we recommend something different from what a major retailer suggests, we will cite the research that supports our position so readers can evaluate the evidence directly.

## Reader input welcome

Small animal welfare is an area where experienced keepers often know more than available published resources. If you keep one of these species and have experiences, research citations, or specific questions to share, email contact@pet-gotopro.com. Your input directly shapes which topics we prioritize.
