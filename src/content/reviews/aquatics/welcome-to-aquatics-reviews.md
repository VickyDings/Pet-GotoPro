Welcome to the aquatics section of Pet-GoToPro. Keeping aquariums is a hobby where product choices have long consequences because equipment runs continuously for years, and the wrong filter or heater can cause a tank crash that kills everyone in the system. We take that seriously in how we test and write about aquatic products.

## Why aquarium equipment reviews deserve real testing

A filter manufacturer's claimed flow rate and its actual flow rate under normal operating conditions with biological media installed are often dramatically different numbers. A heater marketed as appropriate for a 40-gallon tank may deliver inconsistent temperatures in a 40-gallon tank under real-world conditions. LED lights sold as suitable for planted tanks may lack the spectral output plants actually need. These differences only reveal themselves through extended real-world testing, which is what we're committed to providing.

## What we're working on first

Our priority list starts with filtration comparisons, including canister filters for medium freshwater tanks, hang-on-back filters for smaller setups, and sponge filter options for breeding tanks and shrimp-safe environments. Heater reliability testing comes next, since heater failures are among the most common causes of fish loss. LED lighting reviews for planted tanks follow, comparing options from Fluval, Finnex, and other brands across different plant load requirements. Water testing equipment rounds out the early list because reliable testing is the foundation of everything else in the hobby.

## Saltwater coverage

Saltwater aquarium coverage is on the roadmap but takes longer to build out responsibly because the equipment decisions are more technically demanding and testing timelines are longer. We'll start with skimmer and reactor comparisons before expanding into lighting, flow, and husbandry equipment.

## Reader input welcome

The aquarium hobby has a deep community of experienced keepers whose knowledge exceeds anything a generalist pet site could capture independently. If you keep fish and want to share product experiences — especially about products we should avoid — email contact@pet-gotopro.com.
