Welcome to the bird reviews section of Pet-GoToPro. Pet birds are one of the most underserved categories in the affiliate content landscape, which is exactly why we want to do this section properly. Generic pet sites rarely have anyone on staff who has actually kept parrots, and it shows in their product recommendations.

## Why bird product reviews need to be done carefully

The wrong cage can cause real harm to a bird. Bar spacing that's fine for a cockatiel can be dangerous for a budgie. A cage marketed as suitable for medium parrots might be too small for anything but the smallest species. Perch diameter, food and water bowl placement, ventilation, and countless other factors that go unaddressed in most buying guides actually matter enormously for bird welfare. We want our bird reviews to account for species-specific requirements rather than treating all birds as interchangeable.

## What we're working on first

Our priority list for bird reviews includes flight cages for cockatiels, conures, and African greys with honest assessments of which species each cage actually suits. We're also testing seed catchers and cage skirts that address the mess problem most bird parents eventually confront, plus foraging and chew toys evaluated for safety and durability with different beak strengths. Pelleted diet recommendations rounded out against whole-food alternatives are another early priority, since diet is one of the most consequential decisions bird parents make.

## How to get involved

If you keep birds and have strong opinions about specific products — positive or negative — email us at contact@pet-gotopro.com. Experienced bird keepers often know things about specific product quirks that don't show up in general-purpose reviews, and we'd love to incorporate that knowledge into our testing process.

Meanwhile, our [bird care guides](/guides/birds/) section will be growing alongside our reviews, covering topics like species selection, nutrition, and routine care.
