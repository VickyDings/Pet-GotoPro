Welcome to the small animals section of Pet-GoToPro. Small animals — guinea pigs, rabbits, hamsters, ferrets, gerbils, hedgehogs, and others — are among the most frequently misunderstood pets in the industry. Product marketing in this category has lagged decades behind current welfare research, which means many products sold specifically for these animals don't actually meet their species' genuine needs.

## Why this category needs a rethink

Consider the hamster cages sold at most major pet retailers. Research published over the last decade has consistently shown that cages meeting minimum welfare standards for Syrian hamsters need at least 600 square inches of unbroken floor space, yet the "hamster cages" most commonly sold fall well short of that threshold. Similar gaps exist for guinea pig enclosures sold in pet stores, many of which are actually dangerously small for the species. Rabbit products often assume rabbits live in cages at all, when modern rabbit welfare guidance recommends free-roaming or at minimum dedicated pens rather than cage-based confinement.

We want this section to reflect current welfare research rather than historical industry practice. That means some of our recommendations will cut against what pet store employees commonly say, and we think that's exactly the point of having independent reviews in the first place.

## What we're working on first

Our initial priority list includes guinea pig habitats, comparing C&C (cubes and coroplast) DIY options against commercial solutions like Midwest and Kavee. Hamster cage recommendations come next, focused specifically on cages that actually meet the 600-square-inch Syrian standard rather than the "hamster cages" commonly sold. Bedding comparisons for guinea pigs, rabbits, and hamsters follow, since bedding choice meaningfully affects respiratory health in these species. Rabbit care content will address free-roaming setups and pen configurations, plus diet recommendations focused on timothy hay-based feeding rather than pellet-heavy diets.

## Reader expertise needed

Small animal welfare research is an evolving field, and many of the best insights come from experienced keepers rather than traditional pet industry sources. If you keep one of these species and have product experiences to share, email contact@pet-gotopro.com. We especially want to hear about products marketed for these species that fail in practice — those are often the most useful reviews for preventing other keepers from making the same mistakes.
